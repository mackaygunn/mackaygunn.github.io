## Overview
**Project Type:** Practical Laboratory<br>
**Duration:** Year 1 Semester 2<br>
**Team:** Group GA 5/6 SF<br>
**My Role:** Engineering Student

A hands-on manufacturing project focused on transforming raw cylindrical mild steel stock into a precise, multi-diameter machine component using manual subtractive manufacturing techniques.

## Problem Statement
Addressing the fundamental challenge of manually converting raw metal stock into a functional, dimensionally accurate part without relying on automated CNC machinery to meet modern mechanical assembly standards.

## Objectives
* Master operational controls and safety protocols of an industrial Engine Lathe.
* Execute precision material removal to achieve target stepped diameters.
* Maintain strict dimensional accuracy and optimal surface finish.

## Engineering Process
Setup of workpiece in 3-jaw chuck, calibration of spindle speed (315 RPM), execution of sequential longitudinal passes with coolant, and continuous verification using Vernier calipers.

## Technical Details
Mild Steel Cylindrical Rod; Target diameters: 20.18 mm and 23.18 mm; Cut Depths: 0.4mm–0.5mm.

## Design Decisions
Utilized sequential shallow cuts instead of aggressive deep cuts to prevent workpiece deflection and tool wear.

## Tools & Software
* Engine Lathe
* Hard Steel Cutting Tool
* Vernier Calipers

## Engineering Skills Demonstrated
* Manual Subtractive Manufacturing
* Machining Parameter Optimization
* Quality Control

## Challenges
Managing potential tool deflection on the 80mm overhang by adjusting feed rates.

## Results
Successfully manufactured the component within manual machining tolerances.

## Key Learning
Gained an understanding of mechanical feedback (vibration, chip formation) and the principles of Design-for-Manufacturability.
