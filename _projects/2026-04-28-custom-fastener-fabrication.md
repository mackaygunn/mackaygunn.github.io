## Overview
**Project Type:** Practical Laboratory<br>
**Duration:** Year 1 Semester 2<br>
**Team:** Group GA 5/6 SF<br>
**My Role:** Engineering Student

A fabrication project bridging theoretical machine design with shop-floor execution, resulting in a functional, custom M8x1.25 threaded bolt and non-permanent joint assembly.

## Problem Statement
Producing specific, standardized, load-bearing thread profiles manually to ensure smooth engagement and structural integrity.

## Objectives
* Fabricate an M8x1.25 bolt with a 30mm threaded section.
* Form a high-strength bolt head utilizing mechanical deformation.
* Ensure seamless engagement with standardized M8 nuts.

## Engineering Process
Rod preparation, edge beveling for die seating, manual threading using die stock with periodic chip clearing, and permanent head-forming via hammer-riveting.

## Technical Details
Mild Steel Rod (Ø 8mm); Metric M8x1.25 coarse thread.

## Design Decisions
Chose cold-forming (riveting) for the bolt head to preserve metal grain flow rather than welding.

## Tools & Software
* Tap and Die Set
* Bench Vice
* Hand Files

## Engineering Skills Demonstrated
* Manual Threading
* Cold Forming
* Mechanical Fitment

## Challenges
Preventing thread tearing during manual die operation.

## Results
Fully functional 110mm bolt with clean threads and a secure custom head.

## Key Learning
Deepened understanding of thread geometry, pitch, and shear strength.
