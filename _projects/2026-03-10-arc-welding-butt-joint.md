## Overview
**Project Type:** Practical Laboratory<br>
**Duration:** Year 1 Semester 2<br>
**Team:** Group 6A6-F<br>
**My Role:** Engineering Student

A metallurgical engineering project demonstrating the creation of a permanent, high-strength fusion joint using Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW).

## Problem Statement
Achieving full-penetration fusion between steel plates without causing warping or slag inclusions in structural connections.

## Objectives
* Execute a structurally sound Single V Butt joint.
* Achieve full root penetration and a continuous weld bead.
* Perform proper edge preparation (30-degree beveling).

## Engineering Process
Beveling edges, establishing root gap, tack welding for distortion control, multi-pass welding, and mechanical slag removal.

## Technical Details
5mm Mild Steel Plates; SMAW process; 3.1mm flux-coated electrodes.

## Design Decisions
Applied 30-degree beveling to balance penetration depth against filler consumption.

## Tools & Software
* SMAW Welding Machine
* Chipping Hammer
* Engineering Protractor

## Engineering Skills Demonstrated
* SMAW Welding
* Distortion Control
* Metallurgical Prep

## Challenges
Controlling thermal distortion and ensuring complete slag removal between passes.

## Results
Successful full-penetration V-butt weld with uniform bead profile.

## Key Learning
Mastered arc stability and travel speed coordination for consistent fusion.
