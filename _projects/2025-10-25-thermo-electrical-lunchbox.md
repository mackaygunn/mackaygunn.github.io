## Overview
**Project Type:** Product Design & Engineering<br>
**Duration:** June 2025 - October 2025<br>
**Team:** 8 Members<br>
**My Role:** Electronics & Control Logic Designer

A portable smart lunchbox design featuring an automated heating system. I developed the electronics and control logic to ensure safe, energy-efficient food warming.

## Problem Statement
The need for a portable, lightweight, and affordable solution to actively heat meals to a safe serving temperature (70°C) without the bulk of traditional heaters.

## Objectives
* Heat 500g of food to 70°C in under 30 minutes.
* Automated temperature regulation with safety interlocks.
* Device weight under 1.5 kg.

## Engineering Process
Thermal load calculation, electronics component selection, Arduino-based logic programming, and prototype testing for temperature ramp-up.

## Technical Details
60W PTC Heater; Arduino Uno; 20mm Polyurethane insulation; 2mm Stainless Steel liner.

## Design Decisions
Integrated a self-regulating PTC heater to improve inherent safety against thermal runaway.

## Tools & Software
* OnShape
* Arduino IDE
* Micro Controllers

## Engineering Skills Demonstrated
* Thermodynamics
* Control Systems Logic
* Rapid Prototyping

## Challenges
Balancing heating power requirements with device weight limits.

## Results
Prototype achieved 70°C in 16 minutes with stable thermal cut-off control.

## Key Learning
Gained experience in cross-disciplinary integration of electrical loads and mechanical insulation.
