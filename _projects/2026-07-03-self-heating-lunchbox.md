## The Problem
Professionals and outdoor enthusiasts often lack access to microwaves or stoves when they need a hot meal. Existing solutions rely on bulky batteries or inefficient insulation that loses heat over time. We needed a compact, lightweight, and completely off-grid solution to safely heat food to a palatable temperature within minutes.

## Design & Iteration
I explored several thermodynamic principles and settled on a controlled exothermic reaction using quicklime (calcium oxide) and water. The design isolates the reactant chamber from the food container using a highly conductive but food-safe anodized aluminum barrier.

- **Core Mechanism:** Calcium oxide + water reaction.
- **Thermal Management:** Integrated a pressure-release valve and aerogel insulation on the outer shell to prevent user burns while maximizing heat transfer to the food.
- **Form Factor:** Modular, stackable design for easy cleaning and reloading of the heating packs.

## Validation
Through thermodynamic simulations and early-stage physical prototypes, the lunch box successfully heated a 500g meal from 20°C to 65°C in under 12 minutes. The external surface temperature remained safely below 45°C throughout the process, confirming the efficacy of the aerogel insulation layer.
